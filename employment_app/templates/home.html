Basic Concept

  • Unemployed job trainees learn programming from free sources and start writing beginner apps in one year, and write moderately well after 2 years. College students majoring in computer science/MIS are ready immediately.
  • Unemployed novice programmers offer programming services to startups with simple app or web-design requirements.
  • Startups offer equity or delayed transaction-based payments to learning programmers.
  • For startups, the purpose is to give them an affordable prototype that allows investors to evaluate and fund the production version of their idea. For job trainees, it gives them real-world learning experience and qualifies them to apply for entry-level programming positions,or to start their own startup.
