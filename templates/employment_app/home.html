Job Training and Startup Accelerator

Jumpstarting innovation and generating new jobs

The Center for Government Interoperability is establishing a job training program. Job trainees will do web design and app development for startups that cannot afford developers or attract investment firms.

The Center’s projects primarily involve government improvement, but the employment program was initiated due to the broad scope of societal benefits.

Background

  • There are thousands of startup ideas that cannot be implemented due to software developer costs
  • There are millions of unemployed, many of whom could learn programming from free sources

Basic Concept - Employment Program managed by CFGIO

  1. Unemployed novice programmers learn from free sources and start writing beginner apps in one year, and write moderately well after 2 years. College students majoring in computer science/MIS would be ready immediately.
  2. Unemployed novice programmers offer services to startups with simple app or web-design requirements.
  3. CFGIO sets up centralized database to match learning programmers with startups, and creates processes to help them work effectively together.
  4. Startups offer equity or delayed transaction-based payments to learning programmers.
  5. For startups, the goal is to give them an affordable prototype that allows investors to evaluate and fund the production version of their idea. For students, it gives them real-world learning experience and qualifies them to apply for entry-level programming positions, or to start their own startup.
